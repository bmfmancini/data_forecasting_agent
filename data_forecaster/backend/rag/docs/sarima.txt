SARIMA (Seasonal AutoRegressive Integrated Moving Average) Methodology

SARIMA extends ARIMA by adding explicit seasonal components, making it suitable for time series that
exhibit periodic patterns — such as monthly sales, quarterly earnings, or hourly energy consumption.

Model Notation: SARIMA(p, d, q)(P, D, Q)[m]

Non-seasonal part (p, d, q): identical to ARIMA — captures short-range autocorrelation.

Seasonal part (P, D, Q)[m]:
- P (Seasonal AR order): number of seasonal autoregressive lags.
- D (Seasonal differencing): number of seasonal differences applied. D=1 means subtracting the
  value m periods ago, removing seasonal non-stationarity.
- Q (Seasonal MA order): number of seasonal moving-average lags.
- m (Seasonal period): number of observations per season. Common values:
    m=12  monthly data with annual seasonality
    m=4   quarterly data
    m=7   daily data with weekly seasonality
    m=52  weekly data with annual seasonality

Identifying the Seasonal Period:
1. Visual inspection — does the series repeat every m periods?
2. Periodogram (spectral analysis) — dominant frequency corresponds to 1/m.
3. Seasonal decomposition (STL) — isolates the seasonal component for visual confirmation.
4. ACF — significant spikes at multiples of m indicate seasonal autocorrelation.

Stationarity Requirements:
Both seasonal and non-seasonal stationarity must be achieved:
- Apply d ordinary differences for non-seasonal trends.
- Apply D seasonal differences (lag m) for seasonal trends.
After differencing, ADF and KPSS tests should confirm stationarity.

Parameter Selection:
pmdarima's auto_arima(seasonal=True, m=m) performs an efficient stepwise search over the full
(p,d,q)(P,D,Q)[m] space, guided by AIC/BIC. Setting stepwise=True dramatically reduces computation.

When to use SARIMA:
- Series with a clear, stable seasonal pattern (e.g., airline passengers, retail sales).
- When STL decomposition reveals a significant seasonal component.
- When ACF shows spikes at lag m, 2m, 3m ...
- When the periodogram has a dominant frequency at 1/m.

Advantages over ARIMA:
- Explicitly models seasonal patterns instead of treating them as noise.
- Produces better long-range forecasts for seasonal series.
- Confidence intervals account for seasonal uncertainty.

Limitations:
- Requires a long history (at least 2–3 full seasonal cycles, ideally more).
- More parameters increase estimation complexity and risk of overfitting.
- Assumes the seasonal pattern is stable — structural breaks reduce accuracy.
- Computationally more expensive than plain ARIMA.

Evaluation Metrics: RMSE, MAE, MAPE (same as ARIMA — see arima.txt).
