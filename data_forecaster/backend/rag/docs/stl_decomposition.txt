STL Decomposition: Seasonal-Trend decomposition using Loess

STL (Seasonal-Trend decomposition using Loess) is a robust method for 
decomposing a time series into three components: Trend, Seasonal, and 
Remainder (Residual).

Components:
1. Trend: The long-term progression of the series. It captures low-frequency 
   variations and structural shifts.
2. Seasonal: The periodic patterns that repeat at regular intervals (e.g., 
   daily, weekly, or monthly).
3. Remainder: The 'noise' or irregular component. It represents what is left 
   over after the trend and seasonality are removed.

Advantages of STL:
- Handles any type of seasonality, not just monthly or quarterly.
- The seasonal component is allowed to change over time (evolutionary 
  seasonality).
- Robust to outliers; occasional unusual observations do not distort the trend 
  or seasonal components significantly.
- Provides a clear visualization of the underlying structure of the data.

Interpretation for Forecasting:
- Trend Strength: Calculated as 1 - Var(Remainder) / Var(Trend + Remainder). 
  Values close to 1 indicate a strong trend.
- Seasonal Strength: Calculated as 1 - Var(Remainder) / Var(Seasonal + Remainder). 
  Values close to 1 indicate strong seasonality.

Business Implications:
- Understanding the trend helps in long-term capacity planning and strategy.
- Identifying the seasonal component allows for better scheduling, inventory 
  management, and understanding of cyclical demand.
- Analysing the remainder helps identify anomalies or "black swan" events that 
  may require specific investigation.

Relationship to Models:
STL is often used as a preprocessing step. For instance, one can seasonally 
adjust data (remove the STL seasonal component) before fitting a non-seasonal 
ARIMA model.
